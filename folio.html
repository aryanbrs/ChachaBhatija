Trading Mistakes to Avoid

1. Lack of Vision: Random Trading Without Strategy

A clear trading strategy is the backbone of success in the financial markets. Without a vision or structured plan, trades become impulsive, akin to gambling.

Solution: Develop a concrete plan, whether it's a technical strategy like breakout trading or a fundamental approach. Test it rigorously before implementation.

2. Ignoring Stop Losses: The Five Types of Trades

Loss management is the cornerstone of trading discipline. Every trade falls into one of these categories:

  • Profit
  • Big Profit
  • Break-even
  • Loss
  • Big Loss

Big losses should never happen. Trading is a business, and the "cost" of this business is the stop loss (SL).

Golden Rule: If a trade goes wrong, take the SL and exit. Avoid averaging down on losing positions.

Case Study: A wealthy trader once ignored SL in crude oil trades, averaging his position. The market didn’t reverse, and he lost everything—home, family, and financial stability.

Actionable Tip: Use a risk-to-reward ratio of 1:2 or better. For instance, if you're risking 20 points in Sensex, aim to gain 40 points. Recovery is possible with one big profitable trade.

3. Overtrading

Many traders fall into the trap of overtrading, taking 20–60 trades a day, hoping for quick profits. Even seasoned traders rarely exceed three trades a day.

Smart Approach:

  • Take one trade. If profitable, risk 50% of the profit on a second trade.
  • If the first trade results in a loss, aim for break-even on the second.
  • Stop trading after two consecutive losses and return the next day.

Top 1% Rule: The world’s best traders take a maximum of five trades a day.

4. BTST and Intraday Trading

BTST (Buy Today, Sell Tomorrow) is a strategy where traders buy stocks intending to sell them the next day. However, overnight market risks can wipe out gains.

Intraday trades are more predictable but require disciplined entry and exit points. Avoid holding positions beyond the trading session to minimize risk.

5. Don’t Try to Prove the Market Wrong

The market is always right. Trading against the trend or trying to force a reversal can lead to significant losses.

Advice: Respect the market's direction and avoid emotional trading.

6. Avoiding Random Trade Tips

Following unverified tips from Telegram or WhatsApp can be disastrous.

Solution: Stick to your analysis and avoid the herd mentality. Develop confidence in your strategy.

7. Selecting OTM Strike Prices in Options

Out-of-the-Money (OTM) options may seem cheap, but they decay rapidly as expiration nears.

Concept: Options lose value over time (time decay), especially if they're far from the underlying asset's price.

Alternative: Stick to In-the-Money (ITM) or, at worst, At-the-Money (ATM) options for better profitability.

8. Overexposing Capital

Mistake: Allocating all your capital to one trade or stock.

Solution: Diversify. Beginners should focus on stock and swing trading before venturing into complex instruments.

9. Preparation Before Taking a Trade

Do What the Chart Says: Base decisions on strategies like the Big Bar Strategy or the 9:30 AM Strategy.

Identify the Trend:

  • Uptrend → Take call positions on dips.
  • Downtrend → Take put positions on retracements.

Trendlines: Use higher highs and higher lows for uptrends; lower highs and lower lows for downtrends.

Checklist:

  • Emotions in check.
  • No random trades.
  • Entry and exit points defined.
  • Stop Loss (SL) in place.
  • Risk-to-reward ratio calculated.
  • Quantity determined.

10. Post-Trade Actions

Maintain a trading journal to track decisions, emotions, and outcomes.

Advice: Avoid carrying excitement or regret from previous trades into new ones. Follow the "Trade Less, Book More" strategy to maximize profits. Celebrate wins responsibly—donate a portion of your earnings for good karma.
